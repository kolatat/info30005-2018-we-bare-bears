WHY

The recycling process has many benefits including:

  • protecting natural habitats by reducing the extraction of raw materials
  • reducing the air and water pollution generated through processing raw materials
  • saving energy used in the processing of raw materials into products
  • decreasing the amount of rubbish sent to landfill
  • reducing the need for new waste disposal facilities
(Source: http://www.getitrightbinnight.vic.gov.au/why-recycle)
HOW

Recycling can seem daunting but getting it right is easy! Just follow these simple steps:

  • Know what you can recycle.
  • Check for recyclable items in every room.
  • No need to rinse; save water!
  • Remove solid food scraps like pizza and leftovers.
  • Keep recycling out of plastic bags.
  • Always separate your garbage from your recycling
(Source: http://www.getitrightbinnight.vic.gov.au/how-to-get-it-right/top-tips-for-getting-it-right)

Paper

(Source: http://www.getitrightbinnight.vic.gov.au/what-can-i-recycle)
STATISTICS

Steel and aluminium

  • Recycled steel cans save up to 75% energy and 40% water than newly-processed steel
  • Aluminium is almost endlessly recyclable

Glass

  • Recycling one glass jar saves enough energy to power a fluorescent bulb for 20 hours
  • Glass can be recycled indefinitely

Paper

  • Recycling one tonne of paper saves three cubic metres of landfill space
  • Recycled paper and cardboard uses up to 50% less energy and 90% less water than usual paper
(Source: http://www.getitrightbinnight.vic.gov.au/why-recycle)
